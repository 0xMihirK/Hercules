Executive Summary: Security Risk Assessment

Target: scanme.nmap.org (Infrastructure Assessment)

Date: May 4, 2026

1. Engagement Overview

This assessment evaluated the security posture of the infrastructure supporting scanme.nmap.org. The engagement, conducted between May 4, 2026, employed industry-standard vulnerability analysis techniques to identify potential attack vectors. The objective was to determine the risk to business continuity and data integrity posed by outdated software components and configuration weaknesses.

2. Overall Security Posture

Current Risk Rating:

C+ (Moderate Risk)

The infrastructure is functional but requires immediate lifecycle management to address technical debt and reduce the surface area for potential exploitation.

3. Top Business Risks

  • Infrastructure Obsolescence (Operational Risk): The web server software (Apache 2.4.7) is significantly outdated and no longer receives security updates. This poses a threat of service instability and makes the organization vulnerable to known, unpatched exploits.
  • Access Control Exposure (Data Security Risk): The current SSH configuration allows for information gathering that can be leveraged to facilitate credential-based attacks, increasing the risk of unauthorized system access.

5. Strategic Recommendations

  • Accelerate Lifecycle Management: Initiate a project to migrate services to a current, supported operating system. Replacing End-of-Life (EOL) software is the most effective way to eliminate the current medium-tier risks.
  • Harden Remote Access: Disable password-based SSH authentication immediately in favor of secure, public-key authentication to mitigate brute-force and enumeration risks.
  • Formalize Patch Management: Establish a recurring maintenance schedule to ensure server-side software is audited and updated quarterly to prevent the accumulation of "technical debt" vulnerabilities.

6. Compliance & Next Steps

While no specific regulatory framework was applied for this assessment, the identified vulnerabilities deviate from standard industry best practices for secure hosting.

Next Steps: We recommend finalizing the remediation of the identified SSH and Apache vulnerabilities within 30 days. A follow-up verification scan should be scheduled for June 2026 to ensure the risk profile has been successfully downgraded to "Low."
