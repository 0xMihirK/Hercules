Executive Summary: Security Assessment

Engagement Overview

This assessment evaluated the external security posture of scanme.nmap.org conducted on May 4, 2026. The engagement focused on identifying vulnerabilities within the public-facing infrastructure that could be exploited to gain unauthorized access, disrupt service availability, or exfiltrate sensitive data. Our approach utilized industry-standard reconnaissance and vulnerability analysis techniques to simulate the perspective of an external threat actor.

Key Findings & Business Impact

The assessment identified critical weaknesses stemming primarily from the use of legacy, end-of-life software components. These vulnerabilities present a material risk to the organization:

  • Infrastructure Compromise (Critical): The web server is running obsolete software (Apache 2.4.7). This exposes the organization to known remote exploitation techniques, potentially allowing attackers to gain full administrative control over the server.
  • Unauthorized Access Risk (High): The remote access service (SSH) is significantly outdated. This increases the susceptibility to credential harvesting and brute-force attacks, creating a high likelihood of unauthorized system entry.
  • Operational Transparency (Medium): The environment inadvertently discloses system details (banner grabbing), which provides attackers with a "roadmap" to customize attacks against our specific architecture.

Strategic Recommendations

  • Immediate Patching (Days 0-30): Prioritize the migration to a supported, modern operating system environment to eliminate critical software vulnerabilities.
  • Configuration Hardening (Days 0-30): Implement "security-by-obscurity" measures, such as suppressing version headers, to reduce the visibility of our internal stack to external reconnaissance.
  • Modernization Program (Days 30-90+): Transition legacy infrastructure to a containerized or managed hosting model. This shift will ensure automated patch management and inherently stronger security defaults.
  • Compliance Alignment: While no specific framework was mandated, these findings violate fundamental requirements found in PCI-DSS and SOC2 regarding the maintenance of secure systems and the decommissioning of unsupported software.

Next Steps

We recommend a formal review of this report by the Infrastructure and Security teams to finalize a remediation timeline. Given the criticality of the findings, a follow-up verification scan should be scheduled within 45 days of implementing the recommended patches to ensure that the identified risks have been effectively neutralized.
